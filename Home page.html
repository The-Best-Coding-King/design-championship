Olympics Horizon

Olympics history

The Olympics have evolved from ancient Greek rituals to a global symbol of unity and athletic excellence.

776 BCE
First olympics

First recorded Ancient Olympic Games in Olympia, Greece.

393 CE
The Romans

Roman Emperor Theodosius I bans the games as part of his campaign against pagan rituals.

1896
First mordern olympics

First Modern Olympic Games held in Athens, Greece, initiated by Pierre de Coubertin.

1900
Women particitation

Women participate for the first time in the Paris Games.

1924
Winter olympics

First Winter Olympics held in Chamonix, France.

1936
Berlin Olympics

Berlin Olympics, notable for Jesse Owens' victories, challenging Nazi racial ideologies.

1940 & 1944
Olympics canceled

Olympics canceled due to World War II.

1960
Olympics televised

Rome hosts the first Olympics televised internationally.

1964
Olympics in Asia.

Tokyo hosts the first Olympics in Asia.

1980 & 1984
Cold War boycotts

Moscow and Los Angeles Games are impacted by Cold War boycotts.

1992
The end of amateurism requirements

Barcelona Olympics mark the end of amateurism requirements for athletes.

2000
Environmental sustainability

2000: Sydney Games highlight environmental sustainability.

2008
Olympics in China

Beijing hosts the first Olympics in China.

2020
Olympics postponed

Tokyo Olympics postponed to 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
